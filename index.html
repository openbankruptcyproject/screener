§ 523(a)(8) AP Eligibility Self-Screener

A short self-assessment that maps your situation to the Brunner-test factors used by most courts to evaluate undue hardship in student-loan discharge adversary proceedings.

This is educational, not legal advice. Outcomes in real § 523(a)(8) cases are fact-specific and decided by judges. This tool cannot tell you whether you will win an AP. It can only help you think through how the Brunner factors map to your situation. For advice on your specific case, consult a licensed bankruptcy attorney.

Privacy: this form runs entirely in your browser. No data is sent anywhere or saved. Refresh the page to clear your responses.

1. Loan type

Federal loans and private loans go through different paths.

2. Brunner factor 1 — Minimal standard of living

"Minimal" means basic subsistence: housing, food, transportation, medical care — not aspirational expenses.

3. Brunner factor 2 — Persistence

Examples that often persist: documented disability, advanced age with limited earning years remaining, ongoing care obligations, severely limited earning capacity in the field of training.

4. Brunner factor 3 — Good-faith repayment efforts

Examples: making payments when possible, applying for income-driven repayment, requesting deferment or forbearance, attempts to maximize income.

Educational summary

Based on your responses, here's how your situation maps to the Brunner-test factors and other discharge paths. This is not a prediction of any case outcome.

Suggested next steps:
  • If a federal alternative path applies, those are usually simpler than a § 523(a)(8) AP and worth considering first.
  • For a § 523(a)(8) AP itself: a licensed bankruptcy attorney is the standard path. Many state bar associations offer lawyer referral services. Some law school clinics handle student-loan discharge cases at no cost.
  • Federal-loan borrowers can also contact the Department of Education's ombudsman for non-bankruptcy disputes.

Why this screener exists

Most § 523(a)(8) self-screening tools online are sales funnels for law-firm intake. This one is not. It's published by a 501(c)(3) nonprofit, runs entirely in your browser, saves no data, and outputs a factor-by-factor educational map — never a prediction of outcome and never a recommendation to file or not file.
